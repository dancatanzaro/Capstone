Mushroom Miso Soup

The base of this soup requires dashi, a stock used in Japanese cooking. You can make your own or use dashi powder, which you can buy at Asian markets. Support your local businesses!

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: ~10 minutes
  • Yield: 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 12 ounce block firm, silken tofu
  • 2 quarts dashi
  • 8 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 8 ounces ennoki mushrooms
  • 8 ounces crimini mushrooms
  • 4 scallions, thinly sliced

Directions

  1. Unpack tofu, wrap block with paper towels, and lay on plate. Place another plate on top of the tofu block and weigh down with a couple canned goods. After 20 minutes, unwrap the tofu, and cut into ¼ inch cubes.
  2. Heat dashi in saucepan. When dashi reaches 100°F, ladle 1 cup into a small bowl. Add the miso paste and whisk to combine.
  3. Bring the remaining dashi to a bare simmer. Add mushrooms and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add miso mixture and tofu and cook for 1 minute, or until heated through. Be sure not the boil the soup.
  5. Remove from heat, serve immediately, and garnish with scallions
